TheKnottingLounge

This website is a living record of the Knotting Lounge.

The Physical Installation
This is a large wooden structure made of:

  • Wooden frameworks
  • A bench where 1-2 people can sit and work
  • Ropes hanging from the frames, waiting to be knotted

In cities designed to optimize away vulnerability, the Knotting Lounge proposes an alternative: a space where slowness, incompleteness, and collective care are actively valued.

Sit. Choose a rope. Tie a knot. Pick any thread, or gather several. Feel free to build upon knots left by others, co-creating this living structure. Let the repetitive work of your hands become a physical release from the rushing city.

Tap the shared thread. The entire structure holds a single NFC chip. Your presence is recorded, not as surveillance, but as a consensual, collective archive of everyone who has touched this piece.

Watch it grow. Woven slowly by hundreds of hands, this soft architecture, Architextiles, will eventually move into public spaces. It is a tactile reminder in a hard city: collective care still exists.
